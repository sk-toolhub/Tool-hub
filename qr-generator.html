What is a QR Code?

QR Code (Quick Response Code) is a two-dimensional barcode that can store various types of information, including URLs, contact details, text, and more. Originally developed for the automotive industry, QR codes are now widely used in marketing, retail, healthcare, education, and personal applications.

Benefits of Using QR Codes

Instant Access

Users can instantly access websites, contact information, or documents by scanning the code with their smartphone camera.

Trackable Analytics

Dynamic QR codes allow you to track scans, locations, and user engagement metrics for better marketing insights.

Customizable Design

Mobile-Friendly

Best Practices for QR Code Implementation

To maximize the effectiveness of your QR codes, follow these expert tips:

  • Provide Clear Instructions: Tell users what to expect when they scan the code
  • Ensure Proper Sizing: Minimum 2x2 cm for print materials to ensure scannability
  • Test Before Distribution: Always test your QR codes with multiple devices and apps
  • Add Value: Offer discounts, exclusive content, or useful information to encourage scans
  • Optimize Landing Pages: Ensure mobile-optimized landing pages for best user experience

Do QR codes expire?

Static QR codes (like those generated by our tool) do not expire unless the linked content is removed. Dynamic QR codes from premium services may have expiration based on subscription plans.

Can I track QR code scans?

Our basic QR codes are static and don't include tracking. However, we recommend using URL shorteners with analytics or dedicated QR tracking services if scan analytics are essential for your campaign.

What's the difference between PNG and SVG formats?

PNG is a raster format perfect for web use and standard printing. SVG is a vector format that maintains perfect quality at any size, ideal for large-format printing and professional design work.

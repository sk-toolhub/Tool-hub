Frequently Asked Questions (PDF to JPG)

What's the difference between JPG, PNG, and WebP? +

JPG: Best for photographs, supports compression (smaller files), no transparency.
PNG: Lossless format, supports transparency, larger files, best for graphics/text.
WebP: Modern format, 25-35% smaller than JPG with similar quality, supports transparency.

How do I choose the right DPI setting? +

72 DPI: For web use, screens, presentations
150 DPI: Good quality for general use, documents
300 DPI: Print quality for brochures, photos
600 DPI: High resolution for detailed graphics, archival

Can I convert password-protected PDFs? +

Our tool currently doesn't support password-protected or encrypted PDFs. For security reasons, please remove the password before uploading. Most PDF editors allow you to save an unprotected version.

What's the maximum PDF size I can convert? +

We recommend PDFs under 50MB for optimal performance. Larger files may take longer to process depending on your device's memory. For best results with large PDFs, convert fewer pages at a time.
